Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about travel insurance

When should I buy travel insurance?
You should purchase travel insurance as soon as you book your trip. This ensures you're covered for cancellation from that point forward. If you wait until just before you travel, you won't be protected if you need to cancel your trip for a covered reason.
Does travel insurance cover COVID-19?
Yes, our policies provide cover for emergency medical treatment if you contract COVID-19 while abroad. We also cover cancellation if you or a traveling companion test positive for COVID-19 before departure. Some restrictions apply, so please check the policy wording for full details.
What activities are covered by travel insurance?
Our standard policies cover most typical holiday activities. For adventure sports like skiing, scuba diving, or mountaineering, you may need additional coverage. We offer winter sports upgrades and adventure packs for these activities. Check the policy wording or contact us for specific activity coverage.
Are pre-existing medical conditions covered?
We can cover many pre-existing medical conditions, but you must declare them when you purchase the policy. Some conditions may require additional premium, while others might be excluded. We have a simple medical screening process to determine what can be covered.
What should I do if I need to make a claim while abroad?
For medical emergencies, contact our 24/7 emergency assistance team immediately. For other claims, contact us as soon as reasonably possible. Keep all receipts, police reports (for theft), medical reports, and other relevant documentation. You can start most claims through our online portal or mobile app.
