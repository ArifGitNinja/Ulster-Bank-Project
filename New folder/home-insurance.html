Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our home insurance

What's the difference between buildings and contents insurance?
Buildings insurance covers the physical structure of your home (walls, roof, floors) and permanent fixtures (bathrooms, kitchens). Contents insurance covers your personal belongings inside the home (furniture, electronics, clothing). Many homeowners choose a combined policy for complete protection.
How much buildings insurance do I need?
Your buildings insurance should cover the full rebuild cost of your home, not its market value. The rebuild cost is typically lower than the market value and can be estimated using tools on our website or by consulting a surveyor. We can help you determine the appropriate level of cover.
How is the contents sum insured calculated?
The contents sum insured should represent the total value of all your possessions in your home. This includes furniture, appliances, electronics, clothing, and other personal items. We provide a contents calculator on our website to help you estimate this amount accurately.
Are my home office equipment and work tools covered?
Standard home insurance typically includes some cover for office equipment used for administrative purposes. However, if you run a business from home or have specialized equipment, you may need additional business equipment cover. Contact us to discuss your specific needs.
What should I do if I need to make a claim?
If you need to make a claim, contact us as soon as possible. For emergencies like burst pipes or storm damage, we have a 24/7 helpline. Take photos of any damage, don't dispose of damaged items until we've assessed them, and keep receipts for any emergency repairs. Our claims team will guide you through the entire process.
