Common Issues & Solutions

Quick fixes for the most frequently reported online banking problems

Can't Log In

Forgotten Password or Username

If you've forgotten your login details, follow these steps:

  1. Click "Forgotten your login details?" on the login page
  2. Enter your registered email address
  3. Follow the instructions sent to your email
  4. Create a new password following our security guidelines

Still having issues? Call our support team on 0800 231 234

Payment Problems

Payments Not Going Through

If your payment isn't processing, check these common issues:

  • Ensure you have sufficient funds in your account
  • Verify the recipient's account details are correct
  • Check if you've exceeded your daily transfer limit
  • Make sure your browser is up to date
  • Try clearing your browser cache and cookies

App Not Working

Mobile Banking App Issues

If our mobile app isn't working properly:

  • Check you're using the latest version of the app
  • Ensure your phone's operating system is up to date
  • Restart the app completely
  • Try uninstalling and reinstalling the app
  • Check your internet connection is stable

Security Centre

Protect your account and personal information with our security guidance

Password Security

Create strong, unique passwords to protect your online banking account:

  • Use at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers and symbols
  • Avoid using personal information like birthdays or names
  • Don't reuse passwords across different websites
  • Change your password regularly
  • Consider using a password manager

Recognise Phishing

Learn how to identify and avoid phishing attempts:

  • We will never ask for your full password via email or phone
  • Check the sender's email address carefully
  • Look for poor grammar and spelling mistakes
  • Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking
  • When in doubt, contact us directly

Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security to your account:

  • Enable two-factor authentication for login
  • Receive codes via SMS or authenticator app
  • Required for certain high-risk transactions
  • Protects your account even if your password is compromised

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common online banking questions

How do I register for online banking?
To register for online banking, you'll need your account number, sort code, and debit card. Visit our registration page and follow these steps:
  1. Click "Register" on the online banking login page
  2. Enter your personal details and account information
  3. Create your username and password following our security guidelines
  4. Set up your security questions
  5. Verify your identity using your debit card
  6. Activate your account via the confirmation email we send you
The entire process takes about 10 minutes. If you encounter any issues, please call our support team.
What should I do if I suspect fraudulent activity on my account?
If you notice any suspicious activity on your account:
  1. Immediately call our 24/7 fraud helpline on 0800 231 235
  2. Change your online banking password
  3. Review your recent transactions and report any you don't recognise
  4. Check your registered devices and log out any you don't recognise
  5. Update your security questions if you suspect they've been compromised
We take fraud very seriously and will work with you to secure your account and investigate any unauthorized activity.
Why can't I see my recent transactions?
If your recent transactions aren't appearing, there are several possible reasons:
  • Processing delays: Some transactions can take 1-3 business days to appear
  • Filter settings: Check if you have any date range or category filters applied
  • Technical issues: Try refreshing the page or logging out and back in
  • Scheduled payments: Future-dated transactions won't appear until their processing date
  • Account selection: Make sure you're viewing the correct account
If transactions are still missing after 3 business days, please contact our support team.
How do I set up a new payee?
To set up a new payee in online banking:
  1. Log in to your online banking account
  2. Navigate to the "Payments & Transfers" section
  3. Select "Manage Payees" or "Add New Payee"
  4. Enter the payee's name, account number, and sort code
  5. Verify the payee details are correct
  6. Confirm the setup using your security device or one-time password
For security reasons, there may be a waiting period before you can make large payments to new payees. You can typically make payments up to £1,000 immediately to new payees.
What are the transaction limits for online banking?
Our standard transaction limits for online banking are:
  • Daily transfer limit: £10,000 per day
  • Single transaction limit: £5,000
  • Bill payments: £10,000 per day
  • New payee payments: £1,000 for the first 24 hours
These limits can be adjusted based on your needs and security verification. To request higher limits, please visit your local branch or contact our telephone banking service. Business accounts have different limits based on the account type.
