Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about stocks and shares investing

What is the minimum amount I need to start investing?
You can start investing with as little as £50 through our regular investing service, or £100 for one-off trades. There's no minimum balance required to open an account. For funds, the minimum investment is typically £50 per month or £1,000 as a lump sum.
What are the costs of investing in shares?
Our share dealing costs start from £5.95 per online trade for UK shares. There are no annual account fees for our basic share dealing account. For funds, there's typically an annual management charge (AMC) which varies by fund, usually between 0.1% and 1.5%. Our Stocks & Shares ISA has an annual account fee of 0.35%.
Can I invest in international shares?
Yes, you can invest in shares from over 30 international markets including US, European, and Asian stocks. For international shares, there's a currency conversion fee of 1% and dealing charges vary by market. We offer real-time prices for major international markets and delayed prices for others.
How do I choose which shares to invest in?
We provide extensive research tools to help you make informed decisions, including company reports, analyst recommendations, and financial data. You can also access our monthly investment ideas and market analysis. If you're unsure, you might consider starting with funds, which offer instant diversification across many companies.
What is the difference between a share dealing account and an ISA?
A share dealing account is a general investment account where any profits are subject to capital gains tax (if above your annual allowance) and dividends are subject to dividend tax. A Stocks & Shares ISA is tax-efficient - all capital gains and dividends within the ISA are free from UK tax. Each tax year you can invest up to £20,000 in an ISA.
