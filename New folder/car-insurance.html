Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our car insurance

What documents do I need to get a car insurance quote?
To get a car insurance quote, you'll typically need your driving licence details, information about your vehicle (make, model, year, registration), details about your driving history, and information about how you use your car (mileage, parking arrangements, etc.).
How can I lower my car insurance premium?
There are several ways to potentially lower your premium: increase your voluntary excess, consider a telematics policy if you're a safe driver, install security devices on your vehicle, limit your annual mileage, build up your no-claims discount, and consider paying annually instead of monthly.
What should I do if I have an accident?
If you have an accident, first ensure everyone is safe and call emergency services if needed. Exchange details with the other driver(s) including names, addresses, phone numbers, insurance information, and vehicle details. Take photos of the scene and damage. Then contact us as soon as possible to start the claims process.
Can I drive other cars with my policy?
This depends on your specific policy. Some comprehensive policies include "Driving Other Cars" cover which typically provides third-party only cover for driving someone else's car with their permission. Check your policy documents or contact us to confirm what cover you have.
How does no claims discount work?
A no claims discount (NCD) is a reward for each year you don't make a claim on your policy. It can significantly reduce your premium. The discount typically increases each claim-free year, up to a maximum (usually after 5-9 years). You can also protect your NCD for an additional premium, which means it won't be affected by a certain number of claims.
