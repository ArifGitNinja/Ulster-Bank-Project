Fraud Prevention

Learn how to recognize and avoid common scams and fraudulent activities

Recognize Phishing Emails

Phishing emails attempt to trick you into revealing personal information. Look for these warning signs:

  • Urgent requests for personal information
  • Generic greetings like "Dear Customer"
  • Suspicious sender email addresses
  • Poor spelling and grammar
  • Unexpected attachments or links

Vishing (Phone Scams)

Fraudsters may call pretending to be from Ulster Bank. Remember:

  • We will never ask for your full password
  • We will never ask you to transfer money to a "safe account"
  • We will never send a courier to collect your bank card
  • If in doubt, hang up and call us directly

Smishing (Text Scams)

Fraudulent text messages may contain links to fake websites. Protect yourself:

  • Don't click links in unexpected text messages
  • Verify the sender's number is legitimate
  • Look for poor grammar and spelling
  • Never provide personal information via text

Protect Personal Information

Keep your personal details secure to prevent identity theft:

  • Shred documents containing personal information
  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • Be cautious about what you share on social media
  • Monitor your credit report regularly

Secure Your Devices

Protect the devices you use for banking:

  • Install reputable antivirus software
  • Keep operating systems and apps updated
  • Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi networks
  • Enable device encryption

Avoid Malware Infections

Malicious software can steal your banking information. Protect yourself:

  • Don't download software from untrusted sources
  • Be cautious of email attachments
  • Keep your antivirus software updated
  • Use a pop-up blocker in your browser

Recognize Keyloggers

Keyloggers record your keystrokes to steal passwords. Stay protected:

  • Use on-screen keyboards for password entry
  • Install reputable security software
  • Be wary of unexpected system slowdowns
  • Regularly scan your devices for malware

Security Checklist

Follow these steps to enhance your online security

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Create passwords with at least 12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using personal information and don't reuse passwords across different sites.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security to your online banking by enabling two-factor authentication. This requires both your password and a second verification method to access your account.

Monitor Your Accounts Regularly

Check your account activity frequently and set up transaction alerts. Report any suspicious activity to us immediately.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and security software to protect against the latest threats.

Be Wary of Public Wi-Fi

Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi networks. If necessary, use a VPN to encrypt your connection.

Security FAQs

Answers to common security questions

What should I do if I think I've been a victim of fraud?
If you suspect fraudulent activity on your account:
  1. Contact us immediately on our 24/7 fraud helpline: 0800 231 235
  2. Change your online banking password and security questions
  3. Check your recent transactions and report any you don't recognise
  4. Review your registered devices and log out any you don't recognise
  5. Consider placing a fraud alert on your credit file
  6. Report the incident to Action Fraud (the UK's national fraud reporting centre)
We take fraud very seriously and will work with you to secure your account and investigate any unauthorized activity.
How can I tell if an email is really from Ulster Bank?
Genuine emails from Ulster Bank will:
  • Address you by your name, not "Dear Customer"
  • Come from an @ulsterbank.co.uk or @ulsterbank.com email address
  • Never ask for your full password, PIN, or security codes
  • Never ask you to confirm personal information via email
  • Never include attachments unless you've specifically requested them
When in doubt, don't click any links in the email. Instead, log in to your online banking directly through our website or app to check for any messages.
Is mobile banking safe to use?
Yes, our mobile banking app is secure when used properly. We employ multiple security measures including:
  • Biometric authentication (fingerprint/face ID)
  • Device registration to ensure only your devices can access your account
  • 256-bit encryption for all data transmission
  • Automatic logout after periods of inactivity
  • The ability to remotely disable access if your device is lost or stolen
To enhance your mobile security, always download our official app from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store, keep your device's operating system updated, and use a passcode or biometric lock on your device.
What is Ulster Bank's fraud guarantee?
Our fraud guarantee protects you from unauthorized transactions, provided you've taken reasonable care to protect your account details. We will refund any money taken from your account without your permission, as long as you:
  • Haven't shared your password, PIN, or security details with anyone
  • Haven't acted fraudulently
  • Have taken reasonable steps to keep your card, PIN, and online banking details safe
  • Have notified us as soon as possible after discovering any unauthorized transactions
This guarantee applies to all our personal current accounts, savings accounts, and credit cards.
