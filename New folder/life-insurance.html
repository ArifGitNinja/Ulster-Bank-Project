Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about life insurance

How much life insurance do I need?
A good rule of thumb is to have coverage equal to 10-15 times your annual income. However, the exact amount depends on your specific circumstances including mortgage balance, debts, number of dependents, and future education costs. Use our calculator above for a personalized estimate.
What's the difference between term and whole life insurance?
Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (e.g., 20 years) and pays out only if you die during that term. Whole life insurance covers you for your entire lifetime and includes an investment component, making it more expensive but guaranteeing a payout whenever you die.
Will I need a medical examination?
It depends on the amount of coverage, your age, and health declaration. For larger policies or if you have pre-existing conditions, a medical may be required. We also offer non-medical policies with guaranteed acceptance for those aged 50-80.
Can I change my policy later?
Yes, many of our policies offer flexibility. You can often increase coverage at life events like marriage or birth of a child (subject to underwriting). Some policies also allow you to convert term insurance to whole life without further medical evidence.
How long does the claims process take?
We aim to process straightforward claims within 10 working days of receiving all required documentation. More complex cases may take longer, but we keep beneficiaries informed throughout the process. Placing your policy in trust can help speed up payment.
