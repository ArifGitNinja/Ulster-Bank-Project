Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about business banking

What documents do I need to open a business account?
To open a business account, you'll typically need: proof of identity for all directors/partners (passport or driving licence), proof of address (utility bill or bank statement), company registration documents (Certificate of Incorporation), business plan (for new businesses), and proof of business address. For sole traders, we'll need your personal identification and proof of business trading.
How long does it take to open a business account?
For straightforward applications with all required documents, we can often open your business account within 5-7 working days. More complex business structures or applications requiring additional verification may take 2-3 weeks. You can start the application process online and one of our business managers will guide you through the rest.
Can I get a business loan for a startup?
Yes, we offer startup business loans for new businesses with a solid business plan and viable proposition. Typically, we'll look for: a comprehensive business plan, personal investment from the founders, security or personal guarantees, and evidence of relevant industry experience. Our startup loans range from £1,000 to £25,000 with competitive rates and flexible repayment terms.
What business support do you offer beyond banking?
Beyond traditional banking services, we offer: business planning workshops, networking events, mentorship programs with experienced business leaders, digital skills training, access to industry reports and market research, and connections to potential suppliers or customers through our business network. We also partner with organizations like Enterprise Northern Ireland to provide additional support services.
Do you offer specialized banking for specific industries?
Yes, we have specialized teams and products for various sectors including: retail and hospitality, manufacturing, technology and innovation, professional services, agriculture, healthcare, and construction. Each sector has unique banking needs, and our industry specialists understand the specific challenges and opportunities in these fields.
